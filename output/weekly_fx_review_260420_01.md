The active macro pattern is still **less one-way USD panic demand, but not a full all-clear for cyclical beta**. Middle East risk continues to support oil and volatility, yet diplomacy expectations have limited the breadth of the earlier dollar squeeze. That keeps **USD** useful as liquidity, but no longer deserving of an oversized strategic premium.

## 5. Key risks / invalidators

- A renewed escalation in the Middle East or fresh disruption around the Strait of Hormuz would quickly restore a broader USD defensive premium.
- A fresh U.S. inflation surprise would weaken the case for further reducing USD cash.
- A more credible BoJ repricing or sharper rise in Japanese yields would improve JPY faster than this report assumes.
- A sharper global growth scare would hit **AUD**, **MXN**, **EUR**, **CAD**, and **NZD** harder than **CHF** and **USD** cash.
- A cleaner-than-expected diplomatic breakthrough would make the current USD cash weight still too high and would argue for faster reallocation into non-USD sleeves.
